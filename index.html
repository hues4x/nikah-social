4. What is Nusantara?

"Nusantara" is an Indonesian term that refers to the Indonesian archipelago. It is used to describe a vast geographical and cultural region comprising the numerous islands that make up the Indonesian nation. The term is often employed to emphasize the unity and diversity of the Indonesian people and their shared cultural heritage across the archipelago.

Nusantara includes a wide range of cultures, languages, and traditions, making it a rich and diverse area with a vibrant history. It often times includes Malaysia, Singapore, and Brunei as they share common heritage and ethnic backgrounds due to historical, cultural, and geographical factors.

The Malay culture serves as a significant unifying factor. The Malay language is spoken across these countries, and elements of Malay culture, such as traditional clothing, arts, and customs, are shared among them. The majority of the population in Malaysia, Indonesia, Brunei, and a significant portion of Singapore is Muslim. Islam has played a crucial role in shaping the cultural and social aspects of these nations.
